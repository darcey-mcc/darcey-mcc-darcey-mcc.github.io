Prototype 1

After completing both my app and website wireframes via figma, I decided the best course of action for the next stage of this project would be to start prototyping. Using the palette and visual direction I suggested in project 1, I finished my first batch of wireframes in roughly 2 days. The end result was not what I was hoping to go for aesthetically, however I felt relieved that I was able to start testing my prototype and move forward with improving certain aspects of it.

Prototype 1 feedback

After presenting my designs to a handful of student testers between the ages of 19-25 who have to chosen to remain anonymous, I was given a large amount of feedback; both good and bad. The students liked the use of colour and felt that it brought a 'fun' aspect to the outlet, however they found both prototypes difficult to navigate. I was intrigued as to why they found this experience so difficult, so I decided to watch each tester use the interface and see where they would struggle. After observing their behaviour, I explained what I had noticed and whether they agreed with my statement. Furthermore, I discovered that the sizing for many aspects of the design was off, whether it be the typeface or the padding between certain shapes and buttons. The second discovery I made was that the user tended to read from left to right, and was rather thrown off by the central aspect of the designs. It seemed that the simpler the design, the more pleasant and efficient the experience was for the user. After these discoveries, I decided the best course of action was to keep prototyping, and work on new designs.

Prototype 2

After taking into consideration everything I had learnt and had been told to improve upon in my first round of testing, I created a new series of wire frames. I got rid of any unnecessary parts of the design which were added purely for the visual aesthetic and cut down the number of words I was using on each page. I enlarged the typeface on some pages. However, I only did this on pages on which this was needed. I created a more consistent overall design by sticking to only three font sizes, keeping the buttons the same colours and sizes ( for the majority, this excludes the homepage). I also forgot to add in the logo to the top right of my pages last time, so I was sure to include this in these designs. I felt rather satisfied with the final design and was ready to start my second round of testing.

Prototype 2 feedback

After getting my testers to review the second round of my prototypes for both my app and website, I discovered there was still a lot more work and dedication that was needed to be implemented to ensure these interfaces were designed to the highest quality. There was a multitude of aspects which needed to be improved according to my testees. Firstly, many of the students thought that the padding between certain aspects of the design such as the 'create account', 'proof of study' and 'select profile' boxes were too small, which lead to the interface feeling cluttered and hard to read. Many agreed that the second round of prototypes was far more revised than the original iterations I presented to them. However, they still felt that there was that extra something missing from the overall visual aesthetic. I was curious as to what they thought could enhance my project, so I asked what I could do to fix this issue. A few suggested minimal yet funky graphics which related to my target audience, some even suggested adding live animations to the website. Due to the tight schedule I was under, I decided that the graphics were doable, however, the animations were not ( for now).

Final

After taking into consideration everything stated in the previous journal entry, I decided to create my next round of prototypes on Figma. This took no more than half a day, as only a few aspects of both the app and website needed to be revised. For the website, I added more padding space to where the testers felt more "negative space" was needed, enlarged the image icon on the profile picture selection page, simplified the designs of the comment and like buttons on the explore page and added graphics to each page. For the app, I added a graphic to the homepage to fill up unnecessary negative space, increased the padding around the username on the user's profile page, added a graphic to the confession confirmation page, changed the button options for when the user wants to either reply and/or like a post and added functional buttons down the bottom of the user's screen as this was an aspect which was ignored in the first round of testing. These were all requests that were made by my testers, and I feel happy with my final results. Hopefully my users will also be satisfied with my improvements.
